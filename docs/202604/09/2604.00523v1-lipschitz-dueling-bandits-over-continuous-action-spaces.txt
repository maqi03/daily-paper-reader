# Abstract 

We study for the first time, stochastic dueling bandits over continuous action spaces with Lip-schitz structure, where feedback is purely com-parative. While dueling bandits and Lipschitz bandits have been studied separately, their com-bination has remained unexplored. We propose the first algorithm for Lipschitz dueling bandits, using round-based exploration and recursive re-gion elimination guided by an adaptive reference arm. # 1. Introduction 

The stochastic dueling bandits model models sequential decision-making problems in which feedback is inherently 

comparative rather than absolute. Instead of observing a noisy numerical reward, the learner selects two actions and observes only noisy feedback on which action is more pre-ferred. This framework naturally models the scenario where numerical rewards are impossible to obtain. The appli-cations include preference based reinforcement learning (F ¨urnkranz et al., 2012; Wirth et al., 2017), online rank elic-itation (Sz ¨or ´enyi et al., 2015), search engine optimizations (Zoghi et al., 2014), recommender systems (Radlinski et al., 2008), Human-in-the-loop learning (Christiano et al., 2017), etc. Further, most of the dueling bandit literature focuses on the setting, which assumes the existence of a Condorcet winner, i.e., an optimal action is most preferable to all the other actions. The work by Xu et al. (2020) consider Lipschitz bandit setup when both scalar reward as well as a choice to get comparative feedback is available. To the best of our knowledge, there has been no prior work that studies stochastic dueling bandits where only noisy comparative feedbacks are available over continuous action spaces under Lipschitz structure, even without considering memory or computational constraints. This leads to a fundamental and previously unaddressed question: 

Can the geometric structure exploited in Lipschitz bandits be leveraged in the presence of purely comparative (dueling) feedback, and if so, what regret guarantees are achievable?

Moreover, standard geometric elimination arguments used in Lipschitz bandits do not im-mediately apply, as eliminating a region based on pairwise comparisons risks discarding potentially optimal arms. A natural motivating scenario arises in human-in-the-loop optimization over continuous action spaces, where both comparative feedback and geometric structure are inherent. For example, consider tuning a recommendation or ranking policy parameterized by x ∈ [0 , 1] d. In such settings, users often find it difficult to provide reliable numerical scores for a single policy, but can readily express preferences between two alternatives. At the same time, small changes in the parameter x typically lead to small changes in user utility, suggesting an underlying Lipschitz structure over the action space. These applications thus naturally combine continu-ous structured domains with purely comparative feedback, motivating the study of Lipschitz dueling bandits. This paper initiates the study of Lipschitz dueling bandits 

over continuous action spaces. Lipschitz dueling bandits naturally occur in many applications (Xu et al., 2020) that include dynamic pricing (Besbes & Zeevi, 2009) and learn-ing properties of material (Xue et al., 2016). We propose LOG -D UEL LI, the first algorithm that provably exploits Lipschitz structure in stochastic dueling bandits. The algo-rithm combines round-based exploration with a recursive cube-elimination procedure that progressively localizes the Condorcet winner. A key design principle is the use of a fixed reference arm within each round, which ensures that empirical preference estimates across regions remain comparable and enables safe geometric elimination. Our technical contribution lies in developing new analytical tools that reconcile geometric structure with purely relative feedback. Prior works on dueling convex bandits (Saha et al., 2021) rely on using techniques like normalized gradient de-scent and estimate the gradients by comparison feedback. Under Lipschitz bandits, one has to provide algorithms that support region-level reasoning (Kleinberg, 2004; Zhu & Huang, 2025) which none of the works in dueling bandits support. In contrast, our analysis treats a reference arm as an evolving baseline that anchors geometric reasoning: by fixing a common reference within each round, preference probabilities can be interpreted as a scalar field over the action space, enabling Lipschitz continuity to be exploited despite the absence of absolute rewards. This perspective allows us to prove that the Condorcet winner is never elimi-nated and to establish a shrinking-region property showing that all surviving regions concentrate geometrically around the optimum. These results do not follow from existing anal-yses of finite dueling bandits, convex dueling bandits, or scalar-reward Lipschitz bandits and form the core technical novelty of our work. # 2. Related Work 

Dueling Bandits and Preference-Based Bandits. The dueling bandits framework proposed by (Yue et al., 2012) studies sequential learning from pairwise comparisons be-tween actions, rather than absolute reward feedback, with the central objective of identifying or competing with the ‘best performing arm’ depending upon the notion of best arm or winner, like Condorcet winner - an action that is preferred over all the other actions with probability greater than half. Existing algorithms (Yue & Joachims, 2011; Urvoy et al., 2013; Komiyama et al., 2015; Zoghi et al., 2014) typically assume a finite or unstructured action space and focus over arm-level elimination or confidence-based selection. Over the continuous action space, with concave rewards, (Yue & Joachims, 2009) proposed the first projected gradient-based algorithm with regret guarantees of O(T 3/4).Later, (Kumagai, 2017) improved the regret to O(√T log T )

under the assumption of strong convexity and smoothness of the cost function. A more general cost function, α-strong and β-smooth was later considered in (Jamieson et al., 2012) which was further gneralized to any β-smooth convex func-tions in (Saha et al., 2021), only for the sign-based relative feedback. A more general preference function was consid-ered in (Saha et al., 2025) with β-smooth convex functions. This paper extends this line of work to the class of more general reward functions, i.e, Lipschitz function which is a significant general setting as considered in the previous works. The couple of works (Xu et al., 2020; Zhang & Li, 2024) do consider non-convex optimization problem; how-ever, they do not consider noisy feedback and hence are not in bandit feedback setup. Therefore, none of the other works considered a general Lipschitz reward function like ours and their analysis is more focused towards a gradient-based approach and do not extend to region-based reasoning over continuous domains. 2Lipschitz Dueling Bandits over Continuous Action Spaces 

Lipschitz and Continuum-Armed Bandits. A separate line of work studies bandits over continuous action spaces under Lipschitz assumptions, where the learner observes noisy scalar rewards (Kleinberg et al., 2019; Zhu & Huang, 2025; Kleinberg, 2004). The problem was first introduced by (Kleinberg, 2004), which proposed a discretization-based algorithm for 1-dimensional functions. Later, (Kleinberg et al., 2008) presented the zooming algorithm for general 

d-dimensional Lipschitz functions and established a lower bound of Ω( T dz +1   

> dz +2

), where dz is the zooming dimension of the near-optimal region. The zooming algorithm adaptively refines the most promising regions of the action space. Sub-sequent work has proposed alternative approaches to exploit the structure of the reward function. In particular, (Bubeck et al., 2011) introduced the Hierarchical Optimistic Opti-mization (HOO) algorithm, which leverages a tree-based partitioning of the space and focuses exploration near the maxima. More recently, there has been increasing interest in improving the space efficiency of such methods. In this direction, (Zhu & Huang, 2025) proposed a recursive variant of the zooming algorithm with O(log T ) space complexity. Other related works consider extensions of Lipschitz ban-dits under additional constraints. For instance, (Feng et al., 2022) study Lipschitz bandits with batched feedback, where exploration is restricted to a limited number of batches and elimination is performed over multiscale partitions. Simi-larly, (Kang et al., 2023) consider robust Lipschitz bandits under adversarial corruptions, designing elimination-based algorithms that remain reliable in the presence of noisy or corrupted observations. However, all the above methods fundamentally rely on ac-cess to scalar reward feedback and cannot be directly applied in settings with purely comparative observations. In contrast to these lines of work, we study stochastic du-eling bandits over continuous action spaces with Lipschitz structure. To the best of our knowledge, this is the first work to combine dueling feedback with geometric assumptions, and to provide regret guarantees in dueling bandit setting with respect to the zooming dimension.
